## Detailed Review Draft

### Memorandum of Understanding

between

**College of Social Sciences, Jeju National University**

and

**College of Fine Arts and Communication, East Carolina University**

### 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Memorandum of Understanding is to promote academic cooperation and educational exchange between the College of Social Sciences at Jeju National University and the College of Fine Arts and Communication at East Carolina University.

Both parties seek to develop mutually beneficial collaboration in areas including communication, media, public relations, strategic communication, culture, arts, design, performance, and related interdisciplinary fields.

### 2. Areas of Cooperation

The parties may pursue cooperation in the following areas:

1. Faculty academic exchange
2. Student academic exchange
3. Joint online seminars, forums, lectures, and workshops
4. Joint student presentations and course-based academic activities
5. Collaborative programs in communication, media, culture, arts, public relations, and strategic communication
6. Development of future in-person academic and student exchange programs, subject to available resources and mutual agreement
7. Other forms of cooperation agreed upon by both parties

### 3. Faculty Academic Exchange

The parties may begin faculty academic exchange through low-cost and flexible formats, including online participation in seminars, forums, lectures, and academic workshops.

Examples may include:

1. Online participation by East Carolina University faculty in forums or seminars hosted by Jeju National University
2. Online participation by Jeju National University faculty in academic programs hosted by East Carolina University
3. Joint academic sessions in fields such as regional media, communication, public relations, strategic communication, culture, and related areas

In the long term, the parties may explore more structured collaboration, including joint research, joint educational programs, or a shared platform for international public relations and strategic communication.

### 4. Student Academic Exchange

The parties may begin student exchange through online, course-based academic activities.

Examples may include:

1. Joint online final presentations for courses that produce student projects or capstone-style outcomes
2. Shared presentation sessions where students from both institutions present and discuss their academic or creative work
3. Collaborative student activities using media, storytelling, design, performance, or communication-based outputs

Future in-person exchange, including visits by students or faculty from either institution, may be developed subject to funding availability, institutional approval, and separate program agreements.

### 5. Joint Programs

The parties may explore joint programs that connect the academic strengths of both institutions. Possible areas include:

1. International public relations
2. Strategic communication
3. Media and culture
4. Regional communication and local media
5. Arts, design, theatre, performance, and cultural storytelling
6. Interdisciplinary programs connecting communication, culture, and creative practice

Any joint program involving budget, credit recognition, travel, student selection, certificates, or formal academic requirements shall be developed through a separate written agreement or implementation plan.

### 6. Coordination

Each party may designate one or more coordinators to discuss and implement activities under this MOU. The coordinators may communicate regularly to:

1. Identify possible faculty and student exchange opportunities
2. Plan online or in-person academic activities
3. Coordinate schedules, participants, and communication channels
4. Prepare follow-up reports or records when needed

### 7. Financial Arrangements

This MOU does not create any financial obligation for either party. Each activity under this MOU shall be implemented subject to the availability of funds, institutional approval, and mutually agreed arrangements.

Any activity requiring financial support, travel, honoraria, venue costs, student support, or other expenses shall be discussed and approved separately by both parties before implementation.

### 8. Student and Faculty Mobility

This MOU does not replace or alter existing university-level exchange student agreements or international exchange programs. Student or faculty mobility involving formal enrollment, credit transfer, tuition, visas, housing, insurance, or travel shall follow the relevant policies and procedures of both institutions and may require separate agreements.

### 9. Intellectual Property and Use of Materials

Any intellectual property, teaching materials, student works, recordings, publications, or media outputs created through activities under this MOU shall be handled in accordance with the policies of both institutions and any separate agreement made for the specific activity.

Use of institutional names, logos, photographs, recordings, or publicity materials shall require prior approval from the relevant party, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

### 10. Term and Renewal

This MOU shall become effective on the date of the final signature by both parties and shall remain in effect for a period of five years, unless terminated earlier by either party.

The MOU may be renewed or extended by mutual written agreement.

### 11. Amendment and Termination

This MOU may be amended by mutual written agreement of both parties.

Either party may terminate this MOU by providing written notice to the other party at least 90 days before the intended termination date. Termination shall not affect activities already in progress unless otherwise agreed by both parties.

### 12. Non-Binding Nature

This MOU expresses the mutual intentions of the parties to pursue academic cooperation and educational exchange. It does not create legally binding obligations, financial commitments, or exclusive rights unless a separate written agreement is executed by both parties.

## 한국어 검토 메모

### 초안 반영 사항

- MOU의 중심을 교수 학술교류와 학생 학업교류로 설정했다.
- 교수 교류는 온라인 포럼, 세미나, 특강 등 비용 부담이 적은 방식부터 시작하도록 했다.
- 학생 교류는 캡스톤디자인 등 성과물이 있는 과목의 온라인 공동발표회부터 시작하도록 했다.
- 국제 PR, 전략커뮤니케이션, 미디어·문화, 지역언론, 예술·디자인·공연 분야 공동 프로그램 가능성을 포함했다.
- 상호 방문형 교류는 예산, 승인, 별도 실행계획이 있을 때 추진하는 중장기안으로 정리했다.
- 국제교류처의 기존 대학 대 대학 교환학생 제도와 중복되지 않도록, 단과대학/학과 차원의 학술·교육 협력으로 범위를 제한했다.
- 예산, 학점, 이동, 비자, 보험, 로고 사용, 결과물 활용 등은 별도 합의가 필요하다고 명시했다.

### 추가 확인 필요 사항

- 양측 기관 공식 영문 명칭
- 서명권자 이름과 직위
- MOU 유효기간: 5년이 적절한지 여부
- legally binding / non-binding 문구에 대한 양측 행정 검토
- 별도 실행계획이 필요한 프로그램 범위
- 로고, 사진, 학생 결과물 사용 승인 절차
