## What Are FIM and HGN?

FIM, the Function-Indicator Model of happiness, looks at happiness through functions and indicators.

- **Driving functions**: competence, relatedness, autonomy
- **Meta functions**: self-acceptance, personal growth, purpose in life
- **Indicators**: emotions, life satisfaction

HGN, the Humanistic Growth Narrative framework, looks at how people organize experience into a story of growth and connection. In this program, we use three simple story moments: acceptance, transformation, and expansion.

## What Is the Photo Story?

Each mixed ECU-JNU team will create a short three-photo story using this structure. We expect about 10 JNU student participants, so each team will include one or two JNU students when possible.

1. **Acceptance**: What did we notice or accept?
2. **Transformation**: What changed or was reframed?
3. **Expansion**: What connection or question became bigger?

You may also use the FIM structure:

- Driving functions: competence, relatedness, autonomy
- Meta functions: self-acceptance, personal growth, purpose in life
- Indicators: emotions, life satisfaction

## Notion Page

Each team is expected to include a JNU student who helps manage the team Notion page. Your team will upload three photos, short captions, and one question you want to keep thinking about.

## What to Bring

- Smartphone for photos
- Comfortable shoes
- Curiosity
- Willingness to talk with JNU students

## Suggested Conversation Starters

- What is campus life like at your university?
- What makes a festival feel meaningful?
- What surprised you today?
- What does happiness mean to students in your culture?
- What photo would represent connection?
